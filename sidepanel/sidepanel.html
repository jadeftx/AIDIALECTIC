AIDIALECTIC

How It Works

  1. Set up your workspace. Open Claude and Gemini in a split-tab view with the AIDIALECTIC panel open for a three-column layout.
  2. Start a session. Enter your topic or question as the initial context. Optionally set a system context, length modifier, and tone modifier in Settings.
  3. Push a prompt. Click the floating arrow button on the AI tab to paste the generated prompt. The turn indicator shows whose turn it is.
  4. Capture the response. After the AI finishes, click the arrow button to pull the response back into the panel. (Or enable experimental auto-capture in Settings.)
  5. Steer the reasoning. Interject at any point to redirect, add context, or challenge either model. Insert messages anywhere in the thread. Hand off to the next model when ready.
  6. Reach consensus. When the reasoning is where you want it, mark consensus to close the session. Reopen anytime to continue.
  7. Export your work. Download as Markdown, JSON, or push directly to GitHub. The full reasoning chain is preserved: who said what, in what order, with round structure intact.
Arrow button modes: Push (outward arrow) pastes a prompt into the AI. Pull (inward arrow) captures the AI's response. Click the turn indicator in the panel header to toggle whose turn it is.
Mistakes happen: If you forget to capture a response, capture it twice, or things get out of order -- just delete or insert messages anywhere in the thread to fix it. Click a message's author label to change its attribution.
Session history: Click the History button to browse, load, or delete past sessions. Pick up exactly where you left off.
Bulk management: Export All, Import, and Clear All are available in Settings for managing your full session library.
Keyboard shortcut: Alt+Shift+D opens the AIDIALECTIC panel from any tab.
